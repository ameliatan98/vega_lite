2016 Olympics in Rio de Janeiro

Introduction

This page provides the data analysis of the 2016 Rio Olympics. “The 2016 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the XXXI Olympiad and commonly known as Rio 2016,was an international multi-sport event held from 5 to 21 August 2016 in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, with preliminary events in some sports beginning on 3 August. More than 11,000 athletes from 207 nations took part in the 2016 Games.”

- (Wikipedia, 2020)



World participants

The map below illustrates the number of participants in the Olympiad by country. Note that there are missing data for some countries due to data limitations. From the map, we can see that most participants are from the United States. United States is the only country that has over 500 participants.

Within the Asia region, China with 404 participants has the greatest number of participants followed by Japan with 346 participants.

Amazingly, Australia has more participants compared to Russia.

If you want to know the total participants in each country, tooltip is available.

Medal Telly

Below show 3 bar charts on the medal telly of the Top 10 different countries. The first bar chart on the right shows the Top 10 countries that has the most gold medals. The other 2 bar charts below it shows the Top 10 countries that has the most silver medals and the bar chart to it right shows the Top 10 countries that has the most bronze medals.

From the bar charts, it can be seen that United States has the greatest number of gold medals and broze medals, whereas, United Kingdom has the greatest number of silver medals. Both United States and France obtained 54 silver medals which is 1 less than United Kingdom.

What is the most participated spot?

The bar chart on the right shows the type of sports the participants took part in the Olympics.


From the bar chart, we can see that athletics has the greatest number of participants with 2,287 participants, followed by aquatics with 1,397 participants and football with 611 participants.

There is a slider filtering option at the bottom of the bar chart for readers to further explore the number of participants for the different type of sports.

What is the gender distribution of the participants?

The pie chart on the right shows the gender distribution of the participants that took part in the Olympics.


From the pie chart, we can see that athletics has the most number of participants with 2,287 participants, followed by aquatics with 1,397 participants and football with 611 participants.
